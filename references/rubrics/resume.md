# Resume Rubric

**Source**: Ch7 (5 Rules), Ch8 (Before/After), Ch17 (McAllister Top 1% traits)
**Perspective**: Hiring manager reading cold, 15 seconds before deciding to keep going.

---

## How to Apply

Two passes:
1. **Cold-read pass** — scan the whole resume as an HM would, before scoring anything. This is the gate.
2. **Dimension scoring** — score each dimension 1–5, then calculate weighted total.

A score of 1 in any dimension caps the overall at 3.0 maximum, regardless of other scores.

---

## Pass 1: HM Cold-Read (15-Second Gate)

Do this before opening the scoring table. Read the resume as if you've never met this person.

Answer these questions:
- What role/level does this person come across as, unprompted?
- What did they *own and drive* vs. *participate in*? Is that clear?
- Does the summary (if present) promise something the bullets back up?
- What's the single strongest signal in the first 5 seconds of reading?
- Is there anything that would make an HM stop and move to the next resume?

Write down what landed and what didn't before scoring. This is the unfiltered HM reaction — it should inform all dimension scores.

## Pass 2: Dimension Scoring

| # | Dimension | Weight |
|---|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Accomplishment vs. Responsibility | 30% |
| 2 | Ownership & Scope Clarity | 25% |
| 3 | Metric Quality | 20% |
| 4 | JD Pillar Coverage | 15% |
| 5 | PM Signal Strength | 10% |

---

### 1. Accomplishment vs. Responsibility (30%)

The #1 failure mode. A responsibility describes the job. An accomplishment describes what changed because of you.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Every bullet is an accomplishment with a clear before/after or outcome signal. No bullet is a job description. Weak action verbs ("worked on", "helped", "responsible for", "drove") are absent. |
| 4 | Most bullets are accomplishments. 1–2 responsibility-style bullets remain but don't dominate. |
| 3 | Mix of accomplishments and responsibilities. The accomplishments are visible but buried among task descriptions. |
| 2 | Majority of bullets describe duties. Outcomes are vague or absent ("improved the experience", "led efforts on"). |
| 1 | All or nearly all bullets are job descriptions. "Responsible for X", "Worked with Y team to Z". No outcomes. |

**What separates 3 → 4**: Every bullet answers "so what?" — the result is in the sentence, not implied.

**What separates 4 → 5**: Bullets show causality — "did X, which caused Y, resulting in Z." Not just action + metric, but the mechanism.

**Red flags**: Any bullet starting with "Responsible for", "Worked with", "Helped", "Participated in", "Supported".

---

### 2. Ownership & Scope Clarity (25%)

Can the HM tell what this person *personally owned* vs. what the team did? Can they tell at what scale?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | "I" framing throughout. Scope is explicit in every role: team size, user count, revenue, or market scope stated where relevant. Clear distinction between what was led vs. contributed to. No "we" bullets hiding individual contribution. |
| 4 | Ownership is clear in most bullets. Scale appears in at least one bullet per role. Occasional "we" but personal contribution is recoverable. |
| 3 | Ownership is implied but not explicit. Scope appears in some roles but not consistently. Reader has to guess at team size or product scale. |
| 2 | Heavy "we" language throughout. Unclear what the candidate personally decided, built, or drove. No scale signals. |
| 1 | Cannot determine what this person did vs. what the team did. Reads like a team blog post, not a personal resume. |

**What separates 3 → 4**: At least one bullet per role that names a scope anchor: "team of N", "product used by M users", "$X revenue line".

**What separates 4 → 5**: Scope + ownership are present together — "I owned X (team of 6, 2M users), defined the strategy, and shipped Y in 3 months."

---

### 3. Metric Quality (20%)

Not just whether metrics exist — but whether they're meaningful, specific, and framed for impact.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Metrics are specific (not rounded to 10%), causally connected to the action described, and framed for impact (relative where absolute looks small; absolute where the number itself is impressive). Every role has at least one metric anchor. |
| 4 | Strong metrics in most bullets. Minor issues: one or two places where a metric could be stronger or better framed. No fabricated precision. |
| 3 | Metrics exist but are inconsistent. Some bullets are well-quantified; others are vague ("significantly improved", "large increase"). |
| 2 | Metrics are scarce or generic. "Improved engagement by X%" with no context on what baseline was. Numbers feel picked for impressiveness rather than accuracy. |
| 1 | No metrics at all, or metrics that are clearly meaningless (100% increase from 1 to 2 users). A 5+ year PM resume with no numbers. |

**What separates 3 → 4**: Metrics include framing — "20% conversion uplift (from 2.5% to 3.0%)" or "₹12Cr in incremental GMV" — not just a bare percentage.

**What separates 4 → 5**: Metrics show second-order thinking — "launched X, which grew Y by 40%, enabling Z business outcome."

**Red flags**: Metrics that duplicate across bullets. Metrics that don't connect to the action described. Any metric that's rounded to the nearest 10 for a supposedly precise result.

---

### 4. JD Pillar Coverage (15%)

Does the resume collectively address every major requirement in the JD? Not any one bullet — the full resume as a package.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Every major JD pillar has at least one bullet addressing it. No pillar is orphaned. Domain-specific vocabulary from the JD appears naturally in the bullets (not keyword-stuffed). |
| 4 | All major pillars covered. One minor pillar has thin coverage but is addressable verbally. |
| 3 | Most pillars covered. One significant pillar is missing or only tangentially addressed. |
| 2 | Multiple JD pillars are unaddressed. Resume reads like a general PM resume, not one targeted to this role. |
| 1 | Resume has no visible tailoring to the JD. Could have been submitted to any PM job. |

**How to check**: Map each JD pillar to the bullet(s) that cover it. Any pillar with zero bullets is a gap — flag it explicitly before finalizing.

**Watch for**: A pillar that only one bullet covers. Dropping that bullet to hit the line budget creates a gap that no other bullet fills.

---

### 5. PM Signal Strength (10%)

Do the bullets demonstrate PM-specific thinking — or could they belong to an engineer, a project manager, or a consultant?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Multiple bullets show: tradeoffs made and why, user impact framed from the user's perspective, prioritization decisions, cross-functional influence, or strategy-to-execution ownership. McAllister traits visible: Simplify (what was killed), Forecast & Measure (what was predicted and validated). |
| 4 | Clear PM signals in most roles. At least one bullet shows a tradeoff or prioritization decision. |
| 3 | Some PM signals present. Mostly execution-focused. Missing the "why" behind decisions. |
| 2 | Bullets read like engineering or ops work. Outputs without context. No tradeoffs, no user framing, no strategy signal. |
| 1 | Nothing in the resume signals product thinking. Could be a project manager, technical lead, or consultant resume. |

**What to look for**: Bullets that include *what was cut or not done* (Simplify). Bullets that state what was predicted and then measured (Forecast & Measure). These are the rarest and highest-signal bullets.

---

## Scoring

```
Weighted total = (D1 × 0.30) + (D2 × 0.25) + (D3 × 0.20) + (D4 × 0.15) + (D5 × 0.10)
```

| Score | Assessment |
|-------|------------|
| Below 2.5 | Significant rework needed — most bullets fail the accomplishment test |
| 2.5 – 3.0 | Borderline — would not pass a competitive screen at a top company |
| 3.0 – 3.5 | Serviceable — passes basic filter, unlikely to stand out |
| 3.5 – 4.0 | Strong — would get a callback at most companies |
| 4.0+ | Exceptional — top-tier candidate signal, HM reads the whole thing |

---

## Common Failure Patterns (from Ch8 Before/After)

- **Responsibility inflation**: "Drove product strategy" — this is a job title, not an accomplishment
- **Metric duplication**: Same percentage appearing in two bullets signals padding
- **Buried lead**: Most impressive bullet is in the third role down, not the first
- **Scope-free scale**: "Launched X" with no signal of how many users, what team, or what revenue impact
- **Skills section bloat**: Listing 20+ tools signals inability to prioritize — which is the core PM skill
- **No metrics in 5+ years of experience**: Automatic red flag at any senior PM screen
