# Ch5: Career Advancement

## Summary
Covers how PMs grow their careers — from IC to leadership tracks, how to get promoted, what differentiates senior PMs from principal/director, and how to navigate being stuck. Also includes Q&A-style interviews with real PMs about their career paths. The Q&A sections are particularly valuable for narrative framing.

## Key Concepts
- **PM growth axes** (two distinct tracks):
  1. **IC track**: PM → Senior PM → Principal PM / Staff PM — depth of product judgment, scope of ownership, autonomy
  2. **Leadership track**: Group PM → Director → VP → CPO — team leadership, org design, executive communication
- **What gets you promoted from PM → Senior PM**:
  - Owns a full product area (not just features)
  - Drives strategy, not just executes against someone else's roadmap
  - Manages up without being told to
  - Builds relationships with cross-functional leaders (not just teammates)
  - Demonstrates pattern recognition across multiple product cycles
- **What differentiates Senior PM from Principal/Director**:
  - Informs company-level decisions (not just product-area decisions)
  - Coaches other PMs
  - Can represent product perspective in executive conversations
  - Known for a point of view, not just process
- **How PMs get stuck**:
  - Staying in execution mode too long — never developing strategic judgment
  - Letting engineers "lead" product decisions by default
  - Avoiding difficult conversations (with stakeholders, with data that disproves the thesis)
  - Not building a personal brand / external presence
- **Career advice themes from the Q&A sections**:
  - "Say yes to things outside your comfort zone early, no to them later"
  - Ship early and learn from real users — don't over-polish before launch
  - The best PMs have a point of view and defend it with data, but update when wrong
  - Build relationships before you need them — don't only network when job searching
  - Know your "superpower" — what makes you distinctly valuable vs. other PMs

## Direct Quotes / Signals Worth Preserving
- On career transitions: "The best moves in PM careers often look weird from the outside and obvious from the inside"
- On seniority: "Senior PMs don't just do more of the same — they do different things: set direction, resolve ambiguity, and make the whole team faster"
- Career Q&A respondents consistently cite: shipping real things, customer proximity, and being technically credible as the three career accelerators

## Relevance to Job Search Pipeline
- **Positioning for senior roles**: When targeting Senior PM or above, use the "full product area ownership + strategy" framing — not "I executed features well"
- **"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?" answer**: Draw from the IC vs. leadership track distinction — be specific about which track and why
- **Interview Q&A framing**: The book's career Q&A format (question → short story → lesson → what I'd do differently) maps directly to behavioral interview answer structure
- **Personal brand check**: Before applying to a senior role, check whether your resume/cover letter communicates a point of view — not just a list of products owned
