# Ch11: Define Yourself

## Summary
How to develop and communicate your personal narrative as a PM candidate — your "story", your positioning, and how to answer identity questions ("Tell me about yourself", "Why PM?", "Why this company?", "Where do you want to be in 5 years?"). The core skill is constructing a coherent through-line from your past to the role you're applying for.

## Key Concepts
- **The personal narrative**: A 60–90 second answer that connects your background → your pivot into PM → your current trajectory → why this role is the logical next step. Not a resume recitation.
- **"Tell me about yourself" structure**:
  1. Present position (what you do now, at what scale)
  2. Path that got you here (1–2 sentences; most interesting parts only)
  3. Why this role (specific, company-connected, forward-looking)
  Rule: end with a hook that invites follow-up, not a full stop
- **"Why PM?" answer patterns** (common + what separates good from generic):
  - Bad: "I love working with engineers and designers" (everyone says this)
  - Bad: "I've always been entrepreneurial" (vague)
  - Good: Name the specific moment or project where you realized you were doing PM work without the title — and why you want to formalize it
  - Best: Show that you've already been doing PM work; asking for the title, not the permission
- **"Why this company?" rules** (matches Ch9 cover letter):
  - Must name something specific: a product, a recent launch, a strategic bet
  - Must connect it to a concrete experience or belief you hold
  - Cannot be answered with "your mission resonates with me" alone
- **"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?" structure**:
  - Don't give a job title answer ("Director of Product in 5 years")
  - Give a problem-space answer ("Working on [type of problem] at [scale/complexity], with [type of team]")
  - Connect it to the trajectory the current role enables
- **Strengths and weaknesses**:
  - Strengths: Pick the one most relevant to the JD; back it with a concrete example; avoid saying "I'm a team player"
  - Weaknesses: Pick a real one (not "I work too hard"); show self-awareness and the mitigation you've built; avoid weaknesses that are core to the job
- **Personal differentiation**: What's your "superpower" as a PM? Every strong candidate has a clear answer — technical depth, user research instinct, data fluency, cross-functional leadership, 0-to-1 execution, etc. Know yours before the interview.

## Direct Quotes / Signals Worth Preserving
- "Your story isn't your resume. Your resume is a list of things you did. Your story is why those things connect, and why you're the right person for this next role."
- On "why PM?": "The best answer shows you've already been doing it. The worst answer explains what a PM does."
- On superpower: "Know what makes you distinctly valuable. If you can't answer 'why you over another strong PM candidate?' in 30 seconds, you don't know your own superpower yet."

## Relevance to Job Search Pipeline
- **Elevator pitch** (`.claude/commands/elevator-pitch.md`): The personal narrative from this chapter is the foundation of the elevator pitch structure
- **Behavioral prep**: "Tell me about yourself" is always the first question — prepare a 90-second version that ends on the hook for this specific role
- **Cover letter opening**: The "path that got you here + why this role" structure maps to the hook paragraph
- **Job Intelligence**: The "why this company?" research connects directly to JI company/product research
- **Positioning check**: Before applying to any role, verify you have a 30-second superpower statement and a 90-second personal narrative that explicitly connects to the JD
