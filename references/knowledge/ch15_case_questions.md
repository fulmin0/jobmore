# Ch15: Case Questions

## Summary
Business case interview questions — the overlap between PM interviews and consulting-style case interviews. Covers frameworks (SWOT, 4Ps, Porter's 5 Forces, 5Cs, AIDA/REAN), how to apply them, and when. These questions test business acumen and structured problem-solving. More common at companies with consulting-adjacent PM cultures (Amazon, McKinsey/Bain alumni-heavy startups).

## Key Concepts

### Framework Overview
- **SWOT Analysis**: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats — useful for evaluating a strategic position; too generic on its own, best used as a starting anchor before going deeper
- **4Ps (Marketing Mix)**: Product, Price, Place, Promotion — used for go-to-market and launch strategy questions
- **Porter's 5 Forces**: Competitive rivalry, threat of new entrants, threat of substitutes, supplier power, buyer power — used for industry attractiveness and competitive moat questions
- **5Cs (Situation Analysis)**: Company, Customers, Competitors, Collaborators, Climate — comprehensive business context framework
- **AIDA / REAN (Funnel frameworks)**:
  - AIDA: Awareness → Interest → Desire → Action — marketing/funnel framing
  - REAN: Reach → Engage → Activate → Nurture — digital product funnel framing; more applicable to PM contexts
- **When to use which**:
  - Strategic direction / market entry: 5Cs or Porter's 5 Forces
  - Product launch: 4Ps
  - Competitive landscape: Porter's 5 Forces + SWOT
  - User acquisition / growth: REAN funnel
  - Quick situation read: SWOT

### Case Question Types
1. **Market entry**: "Should Company X enter Market Y?"
   - Framework: Market size → Company fit → Competition → Entry barriers → Recommendation
2. **Profitability decline**: "Company X's revenue dropped 20% — why and what would you do?"
   - Framework: Revenue = Volume × Price; segment by product/geo/segment; diagnose before prescribing
3. **Pricing strategy**: "How would you price Product X?"
   - Framework: Cost-based vs. value-based vs. competitive; customer willingness to pay; anchoring
4. **Partnership / M&A**: "Should Company X acquire Company Y?"
   - Framework: Strategic fit, financial value, integration cost, alternatives
5. **New product launch**: "How would you launch Feature X?"
   - Framework: Target segment → Value prop → GTM channels → Success metrics → Rollout plan

### Key Case-Solving Principles
- **Hypothesis-led**: State your hypothesis first, then test it. Don't explore without a direction.
- **Structure before depth**: Lay out your framework before diving into any one part. Say "I'd look at three things: X, Y, Z" before analyzing X.
- **Quantify**: Even rough numbers are better than none. Estimate market size, revenue impact, cost.
- **Acknowledge tradeoffs**: Every recommendation has a downside. Name it.
- **Clear recommendation**: End with a crisp "I would / would not" — not "it depends" without a lean

### Profitability Decline Diagnostic Framework
```
Revenue decline?
  ├── Volume: fewer customers, fewer purchases per customer, smaller basket size?
  └── Price: discounting, mix shift, competitive pricing pressure?
Cost increase?
  ├── COGS: raw materials, production, logistics?
  └── OpEx: headcount, marketing spend, overhead?
```

## Direct Quotes / Signals Worth Preserving
- "A framework isn't an answer. It's a structure for getting to an answer. Candidates who recite SWOT without applying it score lower than candidates who apply one relevant dimension deeply."
- On hypothesis-led thinking: "Don't go fishing. Know what you're looking for before you start asking questions."
- On recommendations: "If your answer is 'it depends' without telling me what it depends on and which way you lean, you haven't answered the question."
- On profitability cases: "Revenue = Volume × Price. Always decompose this first. Then segment."

## Relevance to Job Search Pipeline
- **Amazon interviews**: Case questions are common; LP framing (Customer Obsession, Invent and Simplify, Think Big) should be woven into case recommendations
- **Strategy PM roles**: Roles emphasizing "platform strategy" or "market expansion" will test Porter's 5 Forces and 5Cs reasoning
- **Business case prep for active pipeline**: For roles where the JD mentions "P&L ownership" or "business model", prepare one go-to-market case answer and one profitability case answer
- **Competitor analysis in Job Intelligence**: The competitive analysis section of `job_intelligence.md` can be structured using 5Cs or Porter's 5 Forces as the underlying framework
