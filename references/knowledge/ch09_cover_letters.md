# Ch9: Cover Letters

## Summary
Covers when cover letters matter (more than most candidates think), what structure works, and common failures. Shorter chapter but high signal — the rules here directly govern cover letter quality. The "story arc" framing is the most applicable takeaway.

## Key Concepts
- **When cover letters matter**:
  - Startups and smaller companies: almost always read
  - Large tech (Google, Facebook): often skipped by recruiters, but read by hiring managers when they're interested
  - Rule of thumb: if there's a text box, write something — absence signals low effort
- **What a cover letter is NOT**: a prose version of your resume. Repeating bullet points in paragraph form wastes the space.
- **What a cover letter IS**: the place to explain context the resume can't — why this company, why now, what you bring that's not obvious from the bullets
- **3-paragraph structure**:
  1. **Hook + connection**: Why this company/role specifically? Name something concrete (product, mission, market bet). Connect it to a specific experience you've had.
  2. **Evidence paragraph**: 2–3 accomplishments that directly map to the JD's biggest requirements — not all your accomplishments, the most relevant ones
  3. **Close**: Express genuine interest, invite a conversation, don't grovel
- **What kills cover letters**:
  - "I am passionate about [company]" without evidence
  - Generic opening ("I am writing to apply for the PM role at...")
  - Longer than one page (the reader stops reading)
  - Repeating resume bullets verbatim
  - Ending with "I hope to hear from you" (passive; use "I'd welcome the chance to discuss")
- **Tone**: Confident and specific, not self-congratulatory. The tone should match the company culture — startup letters can be direct and bold; large-company letters should be measured and evidence-led
- **Personalization signal**: The #1 differentiator between a good and a generic cover letter is specific company knowledge. Name a product, a strategy shift, a recent launch — not just "your mission resonates with me"

## Direct Quotes / Signals Worth Preserving
- "A cover letter that says 'I am passionate about technology' tells me nothing. A cover letter that says 'I read the post-IPO shareholder letter and your bet on X aligns with my work on Y' tells me everything."
- On structure: "One page. Three paragraphs. Hook, evidence, close. That's it."
- "Don't repeat your resume. Explain what your resume can't."

## Relevance to Job Search Pipeline
- **Cover letter workflow** (`prompts/cover_letter.md`): The 3-paragraph structure from this chapter is the foundation — hook from `job_intelligence.md` company research, evidence from approved resume bullets, close with specific interest signal
- **"Why this company?" rule**: Never use generic enthusiasm language — must name a specific product, strategy, or market position and connect it to a concrete experience
- **Cover letter gating**: Matches the pipeline rule that cover letter is drafted after resume content is approved — the evidence paragraph draws from the approved bullets
- **Anti-patterns to check**: Before submitting any cover letter, scan for: "passionate about", generic opening, resume repetition, over-one-page, passive close
